Competitive Analysis

Competitive Comparison Chart

To gain deeper insights into our users and refine our approach, I conducted a thorough analysis of three products with similar goals to a music platform.

Competitive Comparison Chart

The Competitors

Through these three music platforms, I discovered that they had very similar formats in how they display songs (Album Cover, Song Title, Artists, Duration of Song, Icon for More Options). On the other hand, where they differ is in their sorting and filtering options.

Spotify

Apple Music

YouTube Music

Competitor #1: Spotify

  • Most Successful: Sorting
    • Spotify has the most sorting options, and even a way to keep the original custom order the user placed songs.
  • Least Successful: Filtering
    • Spotify lacks a lot of filtering aspects within playlists. They tend to just create new playlists based on filters (liked songs, artists, genre, etc.), rather than modify the current state of the playlist.

Competitor #2: Apple Music

  • Most Successful: Filtering
    • Apple Music has a handful of filtering options that automatically modify the state of the current playlist.
  • Least Successful: Sorting
    • Apple Music only offers one sorting option, and it's only by song title.

Competitor #3: YouTube Music

  • Most Successful: Favorite Songs
    • YouTube Music has a strong system in favoriting songs, as it's similar to the use process on the YouTube platform.
  • Least Successful: Sorting and Filtering
    • YouTube Music doesn't seem to offer any filtering or sorting of the playlists, not even searching by song title.

Main Takeaway

Based on these observation, my own music interface will feature both a favorite aggregator and also multiple filtering and sorting options.

Brainstorming Features

  • Favorite Song Aggregator
    • This feature worked well across all competitors. We plan to implement this where the user can see the playlist in live action when liking songs. We can store these in a data structure, such as a hashmap or a list.
  • Filtering by Artist Name and Album Title
    • These features have also been successful and common in most of the competitors. Filtering by these characteristics adds more visibility on what artists and albums the user likes more. We can utilize tags from hashmaps to organize the data.
  • Sorting by Song Title
    • This feature has also been successful in other competitors and is even the default in most music libraries. We can also retrieve data from a hashmap or an array and sort accordingly alphabetically.

Functionality

Intentions

Our app's goal is to provide users a visual way to see how long a playlist last for certain events. For instance, if a users want to create a playlist that has enough songs (without any repeated songs) for 1 hour road trip, then our app can help. Also, if a user wants to create a playlist for exactly 15 minutes, they can see what songs to add/remove to reach that goal.

We use aggregation in our app by storing the added songs from an "Add to Timed Playlist" button. Our app is able to filter by 1) Artist Name and 2) Album Title, and also sort by Song Title.
